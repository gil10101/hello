# Analysis Notes

A few things worth knowing before you read too much into the Chinook numbers. This is a synthetic
sample, and its shape rewards some questions and defeats others.

**The 35.5% coincidence.** Rock is the top genre at **$826.65** in revenue, and the USA plus Canada
together account for **$827.02**. Those two totals land within **$0.37** of each other, and each is
almost exactly **35.5%** of the store's $2,328.60. It reads like a planted signal, but it is just
where the sample happens to fall.

**Revenue is flat by construction.** Yearly totals stay inside a narrow band for all five years —
$449.46 (2021), $481.45 (2022), $469.58 (2023), $477.53 (2024), $450.58 (2025). There is no growth
trend to find, so the interesting story is *mix* (which genres and countries), not *trajectory*.

**Retention is synthetic.** **58 of 59** customers have exactly **7 invoices**; the one remaining
customer has 6. That makes churn analysis degenerate — nobody meaningfully lapses. The useful lens
is value concentration instead, and even that is gentle: the **top 10 customers are only 19.4%** of
revenue, and lifetime value sits in a tight **$36.64–$49.62** band.

**Catalog skew.** The track catalog is lopsided toward a few prolific artists. Iron Maiden alone
contributes **213 of 3,503 tracks** across **21 albums** — the single largest presence in the store.
